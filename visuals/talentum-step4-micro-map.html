A. Today Manual Talent Mapping 15–20 days
01
Brief & mandate intake
Partner receives the brief from the client (or relays from intake). Mandate translated into a target candidate profile in the partner's head, on a Word doc, or shared verbally with the research team.
02
Manual sourcing — referral-led
CMF Sergio Tamez (research contractor) sourced 48 of 85 candidates — 56% of the entire log was referral-driven. Remaining 37 came from Alex Rivera, Alejandro Marin, and LinkedIn.
03
CV intake & Excel data entry
M. Suarez collects CVs and contact information. Manually populates rows into Talentum's spreadsheet template — name, company, title, email, phone, location, comments. Hours per batch.
04
Manual deduplication & cleanup
Cross-checking against existing logs, fixing typos, reconciling spelling variants, standardizing titles. The "Guilerhme" / "Guilherme" type of artifact accumulates over time.
05
Partner review & status assignment
Alex and Alejandro work through the list candidate by candidate. Status tags applied: Sales Rep Rejected, Candidate Not Interested, Presented, etc. Most filtering effort happens here, after the data has already been collected.
06
Outreach — one at a time
CMF From the 85-row pool, only 4 candidates reached "Presented" status over the multi-month cycle. The funnel narrows steeply due to outreach throughput limits.
Outcome — CMF as it ran
85 candidates surfaced · 48 from one contractor · 4 presented · multi-month cycle
B. Today, with AI AI-Augmented Talent Mapping 1–2 days
01
Structured intake form — 5 keywords
JD parsed automatically. Must-haves extracted into a structured intake form. For V2: 5 keywords identified (marketing leadership, retail/CPG sector, MX market, P&L, team scale). Same shape that fits Talentum's existing template.
02
Apollo company-domain search
CMF Target companies defined (Rain Bird, Netafim, Toro, Hunter). Apollo returned 177 contacts — the entire international staff at the four target companies. V2 498 candidates sourced from MX retail/CPG marketing leadership pool.
03
Multi-pass enrichment
Each candidate enriched with verified email, LinkedIn URL, full employment history, tenure data, and location. Wave 1: 12/12 verified emails. Wave 2: 5/6 verified emails. Apollo's data was the spine; cross-referenced against existing log automatically.
04
Cross-reference vs. existing log
CMF Of the 177 returned, 24 overlapped with the existing 85-row log (71% friction = mostly Sales Rep Rejected and Not Interested) — AI surfaced them, the engine flagged the overlap, they were excluded automatically. Net-new yield: 153.
05
Stability & scoring algorithm
Each candidate scored: keyword match count, tenure stability tier, recency of role, geography fit, seniority alignment. Ranked output produced. Comments column populated automatically with the rationale — not a generic blurb.
06
Deliverable in Talentum's exact template
V2 16 final + 3 supplementary in the Marketing Manager intake format you use. CMF 17 net-new appended to the existing 85 rows in the same Int. Sales Mgr sheet. No re-formatting needed — opens straight into your workflow.
Outcome — CMF re-run, this weekend
177 returned · 24 cross-ref overlaps caught · 17 net-new ranked · under 36 hours

Phase 2 — The Architecture Replicates
Same machine, different step. The architecture you saw in Step 4 extends across the SOP.
Step 4 is the proof point because it's the highest-volume, most data-heavy step — if AI works there, the rest follow more easily, not less. The same engine (intake parsing · structured search · enrichment · cross-reference · scoring · deliverable formatting) re-points cleanly at the next four. Three weeks per step to deploy. Each one compounds.
Next — Step 03
Position Intake
AI captures the intake meeting in real time, transcribes bilingually, extracts must-haves vs. nice-to-haves, drafts the intake document, flags ambiguities for the partner to resolve before the deep-dive ends.
Next — Step 05
Initial Screening
AI runs first-touch outreach in Spanish or English at scale, validates interest and eligibility, captures responses, surfaces the candidates worth the partner's time. Edge cases escalated.
Next — Step 08
Client–Candidate Interview Mgmt

AI runs the entire Day 1 → Week 1 → Month 1 → Quarter → Semester → Year cadence. Risk signals surfaced. Warranty triggers flagged. Partner intervenes only on escalation.
